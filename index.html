Sharp Decline In Reading Scores Among U.S. Students, Report Shows, Undoing Two Decades of Progress

Pandemic school and library closures have thwarted twenty years of gains in national reading scores. Scores dropped three points from 2019 to 2022, a plunge not seen since 2002.

By Hannah-Kathryn Valles
Dec. 20, 2022
A report released in October by the National Assessment of Educational Progress confirmed what many educators and parents suspected—children’s literacy suffered during the pandemic.

The assessment indicated that average reading scores declined in students at the fourth-grade level from 2019 to 2022. The report shows a three-point decrease in, an unprecedented drop...

As the pandemic wears on and educators scramble to close learning gaps created by school and library closures, some states face higher barriers to achieving age-appropriate literacy in students than others.

As educators contend with pandemic learning loss in their classrooms, Kanika Mobley, literacy specialist and program manager at Literacy Trust, said parents and teachers are largely unsupported.

“We’re trying to hold teachers accountable but we’re not providing them with the support they need to actually develop successful readers,” she said.
